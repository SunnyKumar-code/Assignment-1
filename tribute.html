Virat Kohli is a renowned Indian cricketer known for his aggressive batting style and exceptional leadership qualities. He is considered one of the best batsmen in the world across all formats of the game. Kohli has numerous records to his name, including the fastest century in One Day Internationals (ODIs), and has played a crucial role in many of India's victories.

Biography

Born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India, Virat Kohli began his cricketing journey at a young age. He led India to victory in the 2008 U-19 World Cup, which marked the beginning of his rise in international cricket. Kohli made his ODI debut in 2008 and quickly established himself as a key player in the Indian team.

Achievements

  • Fastest century in ODIs
  • Most runs in a single IPL season
  • Leading run-scorer in T20 Internationals
  • ICC Cricketer of the Year 2017 and 2018

Legacy

Virat Kohli's passion for the game and dedication to fitness have made him a role model for aspiring cricketers around the world. His contributions to Indian cricket have solidified his place as one of the all-time greats in the sport.
